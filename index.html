The Zeno Paradox in Quantum Mechanics

Albert Trullols Puigcerver

Supervised by Bruno Julia Díaz and Héctor Briongos Merino

Abstract

The Quantum Zeno Effect describes the inhibition of quantum state transitions through frequent measurements, effectively freezing the system's evolution in the limit of continuous observation. Quantum Zeno Dynamics (QZD) generalizes this concept by restricting the evolution to a specific subspace of the Hilbert space. In this work, we explore QZD under repeated position and momentum measurements, modeled using von Neumann projections. Position measurements on a free particle lead to confinement and momentum reversals at the boundaries of the projected region. Conversely, momentum measurements on a particle in a symmetric potential give rise to a novel phenomenon: equipotential translocation, wherein the wave packet appears to "jump" between regions of equal potential.
